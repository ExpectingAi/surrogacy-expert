    Surrogacy Laws in Wisconsin: Guide for Intended Parents

    Wisconsin is a complex surrogacy state. Wisconsin Statute § 69.14(1)(h) creates significant challenges for intended parents by requiring the birth mother to be named on the birth certificate, making pre-birth orders effectively unavailable. Wisconsin surrogates commonly deliver in Illinois, which borders Wisconsin and has a comprehensive surrogacy statute.

    Wisconsin's Birth Certificate Problem

    Wisconsin Statute § 69.14(1)(h) requires the birth mother (the surrogate) to be listed on the birth certificate. This effectively prevents pre-birth orders from functioning as they do in surrogacy-friendly states. Even if a court were willing to issue a parentage order, the birth registration system requires the surrogate's name on the initial certificate. Intended parents then face a post-birth parentage proceeding to correct the certificate — a process that is more complex and uncertain in Wisconsin than in statute-based states.

    What Wisconsin Surrogates Should Do

    Delivering in Illinois is the standard solution for Wisconsin surrogates. The Illinois Gestational Surrogacy Act (2005) provides a clear statutory framework, routine pre-birth orders for all family types including LGBTQ+ families, and strong legal protections for all parties. Southern Wisconsin surrogates can deliver in Chicago-area hospitals without major logistical difficulty. Milwaukee-area surrogates are particularly well-positioned for Illinois delivery given proximity to northern Illinois.

    Minnesota as an Alternative

    Wisconsin surrogates in the western part of the state near Minneapolis may consider Minnesota delivery. Hennepin County (Minneapolis) and Ramsey County (St. Paul) courts have surrogacy experience and have issued PBOs, though Minnesota's framework is case-law-based rather than statutory.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Wisconsin's birth certificate statute makes the state particularly challenging for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families. Illinois delivery is strongly recommended for all LGBTQ+ families with Wisconsin surrogates.

    Key Steps

    If your surrogate is in Wisconsin, begin by retaining attorneys licensed in Wisconsin and Illinois (or Minnesota, if applicable). The delivery state's law governs your parentage order. An experienced Wisconsin surrogacy attorney will coordinate the cross-border delivery logistics.

    Illinois delivery is the standard path for Wisconsin surrogates. The Illinois GSA provides everything Wisconsin cannot — a clear statute, routine PBOs, and strong protections for all family types.
