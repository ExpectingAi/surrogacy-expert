    Surrogacy Laws in Missouri: Guide for Intended Parents

    Missouri is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law with no dedicated statute. Pre-birth orders have been issued by courts in the St. Louis and Kansas City metro areas. For St. Louis-area surrogates, Illinois delivery across the state line provides significantly stronger legal protections under the 2005 Gestational Surrogacy Act.

    Legal Framework

    Missouri has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Missouri's general parentage law (Missouri Revised Statutes Chapter 210). Missouri courts have issued parentage orders for intended parents, but the process is not uniform statewide. Courts in St. Louis City, St. Louis County, and Jackson County (Kansas City) have more experience with surrogacy. Rural Missouri courts often do not. Missouri's broader political and legal climate creates more uncertainty for LGBTQ+ families in particular.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Missouri

    Pre-birth orders have been issued in Missouri in the St. Louis and Kansas City metro areas. However, PBOs are not routinely available statewide — their availability depends on the county, the judge, and the specific facts of the case. Some Missouri courts issue post-birth parentage orders as a matter of practice. For St. Louis-area surrogates, delivering at a hospital on the Illinois side of the St. Louis metro (such as in St. Clair or Madison County, Illinois) can provide access to Illinois courts and the much stronger Illinois Gestational Surrogacy Act framework. This is a commonly used strategy by experienced Missouri surrogacy attorneys.

    Missouri vs. Illinois: The Key Comparison

    Illinois enacted the Gestational Surrogacy Act in 2005, providing explicit statutory authorization, clear process requirements, and routine pre-birth orders for all intended parent configurations including LGBTQ+ families. For Missouri surrogates in the St. Louis area, delivering across the river in Illinois can transform a moderate-complexity legal situation into a straightforward one. Discuss this option with your attorney before finalizing a delivery plan.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Missouri does not have explicit statutory protections for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families. The legal environment for LGBTQ+ intended parents is more uncertain in Missouri than in Illinois or other statute-based states. Same-sex couples have obtained parentage orders in Missouri, but the process is less predictable. For LGBTQ+ families, Illinois delivery is strongly recommended whenever logistically feasible. Within Missouri, St. Louis City courts have more experience with LGBTQ+ parentage matters than rural courts.

    Missouri's Surrogacy Community

    Missouri has a meaningful surrogacy community, particularly in the St. Louis and Kansas City metro areas. Several fertility clinics operate in both cities, and there are experienced reproductive law practices in both markets. Surrogate compensation in Missouri is typically moderate, and the overall cost of living is lower than coastal markets — making Missouri journeys cost-effective for intended parents already connected to Missouri surrogates.

    Key Steps for Missouri Intended Parents

    Retain a Missouri-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your surrogate match. If your surrogate is in the St. Louis metro, discuss Illinois delivery as an option from the start. Your attorney will assess the county, draft or review the Gestational Carrier Agreement, coordinate with the fertility clinic, and manage the parentage order process. Both parties must have independent legal representation.

    For St. Louis-area surrogates, Illinois delivery is the most legally efficient option and widely used by experienced Missouri surrogacy attorneys. For Kansas City deliveries, Jackson County courts have surrogacy experience.
