Legal Framework in South Dakota

Primary law: No statute, restrictive environment

South Dakota has no surrogacy statute and the legal environment is restrictive. Consult a reproductive attorney before pursuing surrogacy here. Intended parents considering South Dakota as a delivery state should work with a reproductive attorney licensed in South Dakota who has specific experience in gestational surrogacy arrangements.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate. Your surrogate does not need to live in the most favorable state — but she does need to deliver there.

Pre-Birth Orders in South Dakota

Pre-birth order availability: No (Post-birth process).

A pre-birth order (PBO) is a court order issued during the pregnancy that names intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital to list them on the birth certificate at birth. Pre-birth orders in surrogacy-friendly states eliminate the need for post-birth adoption proceedings and provide the clearest legal path to parenthood.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in South Dakota

LGBTQ+ friendly: Limited. Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in South Dakota are less certain than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples or single parents should seek specific legal guidance from a South Dakota-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing South Dakota as a delivery state.

Working with a Surrogate in South Dakota

If your surrogate lives in South Dakota, you'll need a reproductive attorney licensed in South Dakota to draft your gestational carrier agreement and file for parental rights. Your fertility clinic may also have monitoring requirements at local clinics near your surrogate's home. Your agency will coordinate the logistics.
