    Surrogacy Laws in Montana: Guide for Intended Parents

    Montana is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law with no dedicated statute. Pre-birth orders have been issued in some Montana courts, particularly in Cascade County (Great Falls) and Yellowstone County (Billings). Montana's geographic isolation and small surrogacy bar create logistical challenges.

    Legal Framework

    Montana has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Montana's Uniform Parentage Act. Courts in Cascade County (Great Falls), Yellowstone County (Billings), and Lewis and Clark County (Helena) have issued parentage orders in surrogacy cases. Montana's small reproductive law bar means fewer attorneys specialize in surrogacy here than in larger states.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Montana

    Pre-birth orders have been obtained in some Montana courts, but are not uniformly available statewide. Your attorney will assess the specific delivery county and advise on the expected legal pathway — PBO or post-birth proceedings.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Montana has a mixed LGBTQ+ legal environment. Some Montana courts have been receptive to same-sex couple surrogacy cases; others are less predictable. LGBTQ+ intended parents should discuss expectations carefully with a local reproductive attorney before committing to a Montana delivery.
