    Surrogacy Laws in Iowa: Guide for Intended Parents

    Iowa is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law. Pre-birth orders have been issued in Polk County (Des Moines) courts. Iowa surrogates near Illinois can access the significantly stronger protections of Illinois's 2005 Gestational Surrogacy Act by delivering in the Quad Cities area or Chicago suburbs.

    Legal Framework

    Iowa has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Iowa's Uniform Parentage Act. Polk County (Des Moines) courts have more surrogacy experience than rural Iowa courts. Iowa legalized same-sex marriage in 2009, and its courts have been reasonably receptive to LGBTQ+ family matters, though surrogacy-specific LGBTQ+ protections are not codified in statute.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Iowa

    Pre-birth orders have been issued in Des Moines area courts. PBO availability varies by county. For Iowa surrogates in eastern Iowa near the Quad Cities, delivering in Illinois provides access to the Illinois Gestational Surrogacy Act and routine PBOs for all family types. Discuss this option with your attorney early.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Iowa has a more LGBTQ+-friendly legal environment than many neighboring Midwest states, but lacks explicit statutory surrogacy protections for LGBTQ+ families. Des Moines courts have been reasonably receptive. For the strongest protections, eastern Iowa surrogates should consider Illinois delivery.

    Key Steps

    Retain an Iowa-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. Discuss Illinois delivery if your surrogate is in eastern Iowa. Both parties must have independent legal representation.
