California is the gold standard for surrogacy law. Pre-birth orders are issued for all intended parent types regardless of marital status, sexual orientation, or genetic connection. No residency requirement. Home to the largest ecosystem of experienced surrogacy professionals in the US.

Legal Framework in California

Primary legal authority: California Family Code §7960 et seq. (2013, amended 2022)

California courts have issued pre-birth orders for gestational surrogacy since the landmark 1993 Johnson v. Calvert decision. The 2013 codification and 2022 updates provide comprehensive statutory protection.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers is what determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate — not where you live or where your agency is located. Plan your delivery state carefully.

Pre-Birth Orders in California

Pre-birth order availability: Yes — pre-birth for ALL family types

A pre-birth order (PBO) is a court order obtained before the baby is born that names the intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital to list them on the birth certificate at birth. California issues PBOs reliably for all family types — married couples, same-sex couples, single parents, and regardless of genetic connection to the child. This is the gold standard in surrogacy legal protection.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in California

LGBTQ+ friendly: Yes

Same-sex couples, single parents, and transgender intended parents can pursue gestational surrogacy in California with the same legal protections as all other intended parents. California courts and statutes explicitly cover all family types.

Why California Is the Top Choice

  • Comprehensive surrogacy statute (Family Code §7960) covering all family configurations
  • PBOs routinely issued for all intended parent types with no residency requirement
  • Largest concentration of experienced surrogacy agencies, reproductive attorneys, and fertility clinics in the US
  • Decades of established case law creating predictable, consistent legal outcomes
  • Hospital systems throughout the state are experienced with surrogacy birth protocols
  • Most popular state for international intended parents pursuing US surrogacy

Working with a Surrogate in California

California is the most popular delivery state for domestic and international intended parents alike. A California-based surrogate or a surrogate willing to deliver in California gives you the strongest possible legal foundation. Work with a California-licensed reproductive attorney to file your PBO petition at approximately 28–32 weeks gestation.
