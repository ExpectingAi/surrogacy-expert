    Surrogacy Laws in California: Guide for Intended Parents

    California is the gold standard for gestational surrogacy law in the United States. Pre-birth orders are available for all intended parent configurations, and the legal framework is among the strongest and most established anywhere in the world.

    Why California Is the Gold Standard

    California has the most favorable, well-established, and comprehensive gestational surrogacy legal framework in the United States. The California Family Code explicitly recognizes gestational surrogacy agreements. Courts routinely issue pre-birth orders for all intended parent configurations — heterosexual couples, same-sex couples, single parents, and international intended parents. The legal process is predictable and well-understood by the legal and medical community.

    Pre-Birth Orders in California

    California issues pre-birth orders for all intended parent configurations. A PBO establishes intended parents as the legal parents before the baby is born and directs the hospital to place intended parents’ names on the birth certificate. PBOs are typically filed during the second trimester and are routinely granted. Intended parents are named on the birth certificate from birth — no adoption or post-birth court proceeding is required.

    Legal Basis

    California Family Code §7960–7962 (the California Gestational Surrogacy Act) provides explicit statutory authorization for gestational surrogacy agreements. Requirements include: both parties must have independent legal counsel, the agreement must be executed before any medical procedures, and the surrogate must have previously given birth to at least one child.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    California provides full and equal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents. Same-sex couples, single parents, and transgender individuals all receive pre-birth orders on the same basis as heterosexual married couples. California is the leading state for international LGBTQ+ intended parents.

    Working with a Surrogate in California

    California is the most common delivery state for both domestic and international intended parents. Fertility clinics, reproductive attorneys, and surrogacy agencies in California have extensive experience with all configurations. Surrogate compensation in California tends to be higher than the national average, reflecting the cost of living and the demand for experienced California-based gestational carriers.

    California is the preferred delivery state for many intended parents precisely because of its combination of legal clarity, experienced professionals, and comprehensive protections for all family types.
