    Surrogacy Laws in California: Guide for Intended Parents

    California is one of the strongest states for gestational surrogacy in the United States. Pre-birth orders are routinely available for many intended parent configurations, and the legal framework is among the most established anywhere in the world.

    Why California Is the Gold Standard

    California has one of the most favorable, well-established, and comprehensive gestational surrogacy legal frameworks in the United States. The California Family Code recognizes assisted reproduction agreements for gestational carriers, and courts routinely issue parentage orders for intended parents. The process is predictable and well-understood by experienced reproductive attorneys, agencies, clinics, and hospitals.

    Pre-Birth Orders in California

    California courts commonly issue pre-birth parentage orders. A PBO establishes intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital and vital records process so intended parents can be listed on the birth certificate. PBOs are typically filed during pregnancy and are routinely granted when the agreement and statutory requirements are properly handled.

    Legal Basis

    California Family Code §7960–7962 provides explicit statutory recognition for assisted reproduction agreements involving gestational carriers. Important requirements include separate independent legal counsel for the intended parents and surrogate, notarized signatures or equivalent witnessed execution, and signing the agreement before covered medication or embryo-transfer procedures begin.

    Many clinics and agencies also require that a gestational carrier has previously given birth and meets ASRM-style medical and psychosocial criteria. That prior-birth requirement should be presented as a clinical or agency screening standard unless California counsel confirms how it applies in a specific case.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    California provides strong and equal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents. Same-sex couples, single parents, and transgender intended parents commonly use California as a delivery state because the parentage process is experienced and predictable when handled by qualified counsel.

    Working with a Surrogate in California

    California is a common delivery state for both domestic and international intended parents. Fertility clinics, reproductive attorneys, and surrogacy agencies in California have extensive experience with many family configurations. Surrogate compensation in California tends to be higher than the national average, reflecting cost of living and demand for California-based gestational carriers.

    California is preferred by many intended parents because of its combination of legal clarity, experienced professionals, and strong protections for many family types. Always confirm the details with California reproductive counsel before signing or transferring embryos.
