Surrogacy for Intended Parents in Connecticut

Connecticut enacted comprehensive surrogacy legislation in 2021. Covers all family types, issues pre-birth orders reliably, and has growing professional infrastructure.

Legal Framework in Connecticut

Primary legal authority: CGS §46b-450 et seq. (2021)

Connecticut's 2021 statute closely mirrors the best practices established in California and Nevada, providing comprehensive protection for all intended parents.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate — not where you live or where your agency is located.

Pre-Birth Orders in Connecticut

Pre-birth order availability: Yes — pre-birth for all types

Connecticut reliably issues pre-birth orders for all family types under its 2021 statute, providing immediate legal parenthood at birth.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Connecticut

LGBTQ+ friendly: Yes

Same-sex couples, single parents, and transgender intended parents can pursue gestational surrogacy in Connecticut with the same legal protections as all other intended parents.

Choosing Connecticut as Your Delivery State

Connecticut is an excellent East Coast option for intended parents, offering a modern statute, reliable PBOs for all family types, and good access to experienced surrogacy professionals in the New Haven and Hartford areas. Work with a Connecticut-licensed reproductive attorney for your GCA and PBO petition.
