    Surrogacy Laws in North Dakota: Guide for Intended Parents

    North Dakota is a complex surrogacy state with no statute and very limited court experience. North Dakota surrogates near Minnesota can access significantly stronger legal protections by delivering in the Twin Cities metro, where Hennepin and Ramsey County courts have surrogacy experience.

    Legal Environment

    North Dakota has no surrogacy statute and extremely limited case law or court experience with gestational surrogacy. The reproductive law bar in North Dakota is very small, with few attorneys having specific surrogacy expertise. North Dakota's conservative political climate further complicates the picture for LGBTQ+ families.

    What North Dakota Surrogates Should Do

    Delivering in Minnesota is the standard alternative for North Dakota surrogates, particularly those near the eastern border. Fargo-area surrogates are close to the Minnesota border; delivering in the Moorhead, MN area (Clay County) or driving to the Twin Cities (Hennepin/Ramsey counties) provides access to Minnesota courts with surrogacy experience. The Twin Cities is a 3.5-hour drive from Fargo and is the most reliable Minnesota destination for PBOs.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    North Dakota is not a viable delivery state for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families. Minnesota delivery, particularly in the Twin Cities, is strongly recommended for LGBTQ+ families with North Dakota surrogates.

    Key Steps

    If your surrogate is in North Dakota, retain attorneys licensed in both North Dakota and Minnesota. An experienced reproductive attorney will coordinate cross-border delivery logistics.

    Minnesota delivery is the practical solution for North Dakota surrogates. Twin Cities courts have surrogacy experience and provide far more legal certainty than any North Dakota court.
