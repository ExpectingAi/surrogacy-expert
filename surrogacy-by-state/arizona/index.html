    Surrogacy Laws in Arizona: Guide for Intended Parents

    Arizona is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law. Pre-birth orders are available in Maricopa County (Phoenix) courts, which have experience with surrogacy cases. Arizona has a large surrogate community and strong fertility clinic infrastructure in the Phoenix metro.

    Legal Framework

    Arizona has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Arizona's Uniform Parentage Act. Maricopa County (Phoenix) courts have significant surrogacy experience and have routinely issued pre-birth orders for intended parents, including same-sex couples. Pima County (Tucson) also has some surrogacy experience. Rural Arizona courts are less predictable.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Arizona

    Pre-birth orders are routinely issued in Maricopa County for gestational surrogacy cases. Arizona is one of the more reliable moderate-complexity states for PBOs in the Phoenix metro, with an experienced reproductive law bar and judges familiar with surrogacy. If your surrogate delivers in the Phoenix area, the legal process is well-established. Outside the Phoenix and Tucson metros, your attorney will assess the specific court.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Arizona does not have explicit statutory protections for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families, but Maricopa County courts have issued parentage orders for same-sex couples. The non-genetic parent in a same-sex couple should discuss with their attorney whether any additional steps are needed in their specific county and court. LGBTQ+ families with Phoenix-area deliveries generally have a straightforward path to PBOs.

    Arizona's Surrogacy Community

    Arizona — particularly the Phoenix metro — has a large and active surrogacy community. Multiple well-regarded fertility clinics operate in the Phoenix area, and surrogate compensation in Arizona is generally lower than in California, making Arizona journeys more cost-effective.

    Key Steps

    Retain an Arizona-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. For Phoenix-area deliveries, the process is well-established. Your attorney will draft or review the GCA, coordinate with the fertility clinic, and manage the parentage order. Both parties must have independent legal representation.

    Phoenix-area Arizona surrogacy is one of the more reliable moderate-complexity options in the Southwest — experienced courts, strong fertility infrastructure, and a large surrogate community at lower cost than California.
