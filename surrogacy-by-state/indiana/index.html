    Surrogacy Laws in Indiana: Guide for Intended Parents

    Indiana is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law with no dedicated statute. Pre-birth orders are available in some counties, particularly Marion County (Indianapolis). For intended parents with Indiana surrogates near the Chicago suburbs, Illinois delivery under the 2005 Gestational Surrogacy Act is often a stronger option.

    Legal Framework

    Indiana has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Indiana's general parentage statutes (Indiana Code Title 31). Courts in Marion County (Indianapolis) have issued parentage orders for intended parents, but the process is less uniform than in statute-based states. Indiana's broader political and legal climate creates more uncertainty for surrogacy — particularly for LGBTQ+ families — than neighboring Illinois, which has had a comprehensive gestational surrogacy statute since 2005.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Indiana

    Pre-birth orders are available in Indiana in some courts, but are not uniformly issued statewide. Marion County (Indianapolis) has the most surrogacy-experienced courts. Courts in other parts of Indiana vary considerably in their willingness and ability to issue PBOs. For surrogates in northwestern Indiana (near Chicago), delivery at an Illinois hospital can provide access to Illinois courts and the much stronger protections of the Illinois Gestational Surrogacy Act. Discuss the county and delivery logistics with your attorney before finalizing your match.

    Indiana vs. Illinois: The Key Comparison

    Illinois enacted the Gestational Surrogacy Act in 2005, providing a clear statutory framework, explicit authorization for compensated surrogacy, and routine pre-birth orders for all family types. For Indiana surrogates who live in Lake County, Porter County, or other northwestern Indiana counties near Chicago, delivering at an Illinois hospital is often the most legally straightforward option. Many experienced Indiana surrogacy attorneys routinely advise this approach. Discuss it with your attorney early in the process.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Indiana has a more conservative legal and political climate than neighboring Illinois. LGBTQ+ intended parents have faced greater uncertainty in Indiana courts, and the state does not have explicit statutory protections for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families. For LGBTQ+ families with Indiana-based surrogates, Illinois delivery is strongly worth considering. Within Indiana, Marion County (Indianapolis) is the most favorable jurisdiction.

    Indiana's Surrogacy Community

    Indiana has a moderate surrogacy community centered primarily around Indianapolis. Several fertility clinics operate in the Indianapolis metro, and there is an established reproductive law bar with surrogacy experience. Surrogate compensation in Indiana tends to be lower than in coastal markets, which can make Indiana journeys cost-effective.

    Key Steps for Indiana Intended Parents

    Retain an Indiana-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your surrogate match. If your surrogate is in northwestern Indiana, discuss Illinois delivery as an option from the outset. Your attorney will assess the county, draft or review the Gestational Carrier Agreement, coordinate with the fertility clinic, and manage the parentage order. Both parties must have independent legal representation.

    For Indiana surrogates near Chicago, Illinois delivery under the 2005 GSA offers significantly stronger protections. For Indianapolis-area deliveries, Marion County courts have surrogacy experience. Retain local counsel early.
