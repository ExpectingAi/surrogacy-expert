    Surrogacy Laws in Tennessee: Guide for Intended Parents

    Tennessee is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law. Pre-birth orders have been issued by courts in the Nashville and Knoxville areas, but outcomes vary by county and judge. Tennessee's political and legal climate creates more uncertainty than in neighboring Virginia, which has a comprehensive surrogacy statute.

    Legal Framework

    Tennessee has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Tennessee's general parentage statutes (Tennessee Code Annotated Title 36). Tennessee courts have varying levels of experience with surrogacy, and there is no statewide uniformity in how parentage orders are processed. Judges in Davidson County (Nashville) and Knox County (Knoxville) have more surrogacy experience; rural Tennessee courts often do not. Tennessee's broader political climate adds uncertainty, and LGBTQ+ families in particular should consult carefully with a local reproductive attorney before proceeding.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Tennessee

    Pre-birth orders have been issued in Tennessee by experienced judges in Nashville and Knoxville metro areas. However, PBOs are not uniformly available and depend heavily on the specific county and judge assigned to the case. Some courts prefer to issue post-birth parentage orders. Unlike surrogacy-friendly states where PBOs are routine, Tennessee requires your attorney to assess the specific court and judge before advising on expected procedure. If your surrogate is in a rural county, your attorney may recommend delivering at a Nashville or Knoxville hospital within a more favorable jurisdiction.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Tennessee has passed restrictive legislation in other LGBTQ+ contexts, and the legal environment for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families is more uncertain than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples have obtained parentage orders in Tennessee, but LGBTQ+ intended parents face greater procedural uncertainty and should have detailed conversations with a Tennessee-licensed reproductive attorney before committing to a Tennessee delivery. For LGBTQ+ families, neighboring Virginia — which has a comprehensive surrogacy statute and explicit LGBTQ+ protections — may be a preferable delivery option if logistically feasible.

    Tennessee's Surrogacy Community

    Despite the legal complexity, Tennessee has a meaningful surrogacy community, particularly in Nashville and the surrounding Middle Tennessee area. Nashville's growth as a major metro has brought strong fertility clinic infrastructure, and multiple experienced reproductive law practices operate there. Surrogate compensation in Tennessee is typically lower than in coastal markets, which can make Tennessee journeys cost-effective.

    Key Steps for Tennessee Intended Parents

    Retain a Tennessee-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. Discuss county selection, expected legal pathway, and LGBTQ+ considerations (if applicable) before signing with an agency or locking in a surrogate. Your attorney will draft or review the Gestational Carrier Agreement, coordinate with the fertility clinic, and manage the parentage order process. Both parties must have independent legal counsel.

    Tennessee has a growing surrogacy community but more legal variability than neighboring Virginia. County selection and attorney experience matter more here than in statute-based states.
