    Surrogacy Laws in Texas: Guide for Intended Parents

    Texas has a specific gestational surrogacy statute under the Texas Family Code that provides a legal framework for intended parents, though it has notable restrictions compared to more permissive states.

    Legal Framework

    Texas Family Code Chapter 160 (Subchapter I) provides a statutory framework for gestational surrogacy. The statute allows gestational surrogacy agreements and provides a pathway for intended parents to establish parental rights. However, the Texas statute has historically been interpreted to apply most clearly to married heterosexual couples, creating uncertainty for other intended parent configurations.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Texas

    Pre-birth orders are available in Texas but not uniformly granted across all counties. Some Texas counties have well-established surrogacy-friendly courts; others are more variable. Married heterosexual couples tend to have the most straightforward path to a PBO. Unmarried couples and same-sex couples should consult with a Texas-licensed reproductive attorney to understand county-specific expectations before finalizing a delivery plan.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Texas presents more complexity for LGBTQ+ intended parents than surrogacy-friendly states. While same-sex couples can pursue surrogacy in Texas, the legal process may be more involved and county-dependent. LGBTQ+ intended parents should strongly consider a delivery state with more explicit protections (California, Nevada, Illinois, New York) if possible.

    Working with a Surrogate in Texas

    Texas has a large surrogate population and many experienced reproductive attorneys and fertility clinics. Surrogate compensation in Texas tends to be lower than in California or Nevada, making Texas an attractive option for cost-conscious intended parents who are willing to navigate its moderate legal complexity.

    Texas can work well for married heterosexual couples with an experienced local attorney. LGBTQ+ parents and single parents should carefully weigh delivery state options.
