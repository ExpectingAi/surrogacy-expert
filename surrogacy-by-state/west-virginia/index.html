    Surrogacy Laws in West Virginia: Guide for Intended Parents

    West Virginia is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law with no dedicated statute. Pre-birth orders have been issued in some courts. West Virginia surrogates near Virginia or Pennsylvania borders may have access to more protective legal frameworks by delivering across state lines.

    Legal Framework

    West Virginia has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and West Virginia's general parentage statutes. Kanawha County (Charleston) and Cabell County (Huntington) courts have more surrogacy experience than rural West Virginia courts. West Virginia's conservative political climate creates more uncertainty for LGBTQ+ families than in statute-based states.

    Pre-Birth Orders in West Virginia

    Pre-birth orders have been obtained in West Virginia in some courts, particularly in the Charleston and Huntington areas. PBO availability varies by county. Some courts require post-birth proceedings. For surrogates near the Virginia border (Eastern Panhandle, Martinsburg area), Virginia delivery under the Virginia ART Act provides significantly stronger legal protections.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    West Virginia does not have explicit statutory protections for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families. LGBTQ+ intended parents should consult carefully with a local reproductive attorney. Virginia delivery, where applicable geographically, offers explicit LGBTQ+ protections under the Virginia ART Act.

    Key Steps

    Retain a West Virginia-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. Assess delivery county and cross-border options if applicable. Both parties must have independent legal representation.

    West Virginia surrogacy journeys are achievable with experienced local counsel. Eastern Panhandle surrogates near Virginia should discuss Virginia delivery with their attorney — Virginia's ART Act provides significantly stronger protections.
