Legal Framework in Colorado

Primary law: CRS 19-4-106 (USCACA)

Colorado follows the Uniform Parentage Act and issues pre-birth orders. Strong protections for same-sex couples and single parents. Intended parents considering Colorado as a delivery state should work with a reproductive attorney licensed in Colorado who has specific experience in gestational surrogacy arrangements.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate. Your surrogate does not need to live in the most favorable state — but she does need to deliver there.

Pre-Birth Orders in Colorado

Pre-birth order availability: Yes (Pre-birth available).

A pre-birth order (PBO) is a court order issued during the pregnancy that names intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital to list them on the birth certificate at birth. Pre-birth orders in surrogacy-friendly states eliminate the need for post-birth adoption proceedings and provide the clearest legal path to parenthood.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Colorado

LGBTQ+ friendly: Yes. Colorado is explicitly inclusive of all family types in its surrogacy legal framework. Same-sex couples, single parents, and transgender intended parents can pursue gestational surrogacy with the same legal protections as all other intended parents.

Working with a Surrogate in Colorado

If your surrogate lives in Colorado, you'll need a reproductive attorney licensed in Colorado to draft your gestational carrier agreement and file for parental rights. Your fertility clinic may also have monitoring requirements at local clinics near your surrogate's home. Your agency will coordinate the logistics.
