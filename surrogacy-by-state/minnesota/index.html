    Surrogacy Laws in Minnesota: Guide for Intended Parents

    Minnesota is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law, with pre-birth orders available in Hennepin County (Minneapolis) and other metro courts. Minnesota has a growing surrogacy community and strong medical infrastructure in the Twin Cities.

    Legal Framework

    Minnesota has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and the Minnesota Parentage Act (Minnesota Statutes Chapter 257). Minnesota courts have recognized gestational carrier agreements and issued parentage orders for intended parents, with the Twin Cities metro courts being the most experienced and predictable. Minnesota's legal climate is generally considered favorable toward surrogacy in the metro area, and the state has a well-established reproductive law bar.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Minnesota

    Pre-birth orders are available in Minnesota, particularly in Hennepin County (Minneapolis), Ramsey County (St. Paul), and Dakota County. These courts have judges experienced with surrogacy and parentage matters and routinely issue PBOs for both different-sex and same-sex couples. Courts in greater Minnesota outside the Twin Cities metro may have limited experience with surrogacy, and outcomes in rural counties are less predictable. Your attorney will assess the specific court based on where your surrogate plans to deliver.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Minnesota has strong LGBTQ+ protections in general, and Twin Cities courts have been reliably favorable for LGBTQ+ intended parents in surrogacy cases. Minnesota legalized same-sex marriage in 2013 and has broad anti-discrimination protections. Same-sex couples and single LGBTQ+ intended parents have successfully obtained pre-birth orders in Hennepin and Ramsey counties. Rural Minnesota courts are less predictable, reinforcing the importance of Twin Cities delivery for LGBTQ+ families.

    Minnesota's Surrogacy Community

    The Twin Cities has a growing and active surrogacy community. Minneapolis and St. Paul are home to well-regarded fertility clinics and reproductive law practices with surrogacy expertise. The University of Minnesota Medical Center and other major health systems provide strong obstetric care. Surrogate compensation in Minnesota tends to be moderate compared to coastal markets, making Minnesota journeys cost-effective for intended parents working with a local surrogate.

    Key Steps for Minnesota Intended Parents

    Engage a Minnesota-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. Your attorney will assess the delivery county, draft or review the Gestational Carrier Agreement, and file for parentage. For Twin Cities deliveries, the process is well-established. For deliveries in greater Minnesota, your attorney may advise on options including delivery at a Twin Cities hospital. Both parties must have independent legal representation.

    Minnesota's Twin Cities courts are experienced and reliable for surrogacy parentage orders — including for LGBTQ+ families. County selection matters for out-state Minnesota deliveries.
