Surrogacy for Intended Parents in Florida

Florida allows gestational surrogacy but its statute dates to 1993 and is more restrictive than modern statutes. Pre-birth orders are available but may require certain criteria. Same-sex couples and single parents have successfully pursued surrogacy in Florida through case law. Attorney guidance is important.

Legal Framework in Florida

Primary legal authority: FS §742.15 (1993, amended)

Florida's dated statute requires navigating gaps through case law, particularly for non-traditional families. Experienced local counsel is essential.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate — not where you live or where your agency is located. Plan your delivery state carefully.

Pre-Birth Orders in Florida

Pre-birth order availability: Yes — pre-birth (with limitations)

Florida courts issue pre-birth orders, but the 1993 statute contains criteria that can create complications for some family configurations. An experienced Florida reproductive attorney is essential to navigate the specific requirements in your county.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Florida

LGBTQ+ friendly: Moderate

Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Florida are more variable than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples and single parents should obtain specific legal guidance from a Florida-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Florida as a delivery state.

Choosing Florida as Your Delivery State

Florida has a substantial surrogacy industry, particularly in South Florida and the Tampa Bay area, with experienced agencies, attorneys, and fertility clinics. The dated statute creates some complexity but many journeys complete successfully with the right legal team. Consult a Florida-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your surrogate match.
