Legal Framework in Arkansas

Primary law: ACA §9-10-201 (limited)

Arkansas has a surrogacy statute but it is limited in scope. Legal parentage can generally be established but may require post-birth processes. Intended parents considering Arkansas as a delivery state should work with a reproductive attorney licensed in Arkansas who has specific experience in gestational surrogacy arrangements.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate. Your surrogate does not need to live in the most favorable state — but she does need to deliver there.

Pre-Birth Orders in Arkansas

Pre-birth order availability: Limited (Post-birth typically).

A pre-birth order (PBO) is a court order issued during the pregnancy that names intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital to list them on the birth certificate at birth. Pre-birth orders in surrogacy-friendly states eliminate the need for post-birth adoption proceedings and provide the clearest legal path to parenthood.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Arkansas

LGBTQ+ friendly: Limited. Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Arkansas are less certain than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples or single parents should seek specific legal guidance from a Arkansas-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Arkansas as a delivery state.

Working with a Surrogate in Arkansas

If your surrogate lives in Arkansas, you'll need a reproductive attorney licensed in Arkansas to draft your gestational carrier agreement and file for parental rights. Your fertility clinic may also have monitoring requirements at local clinics near your surrogate's home. Your agency will coordinate the logistics.
