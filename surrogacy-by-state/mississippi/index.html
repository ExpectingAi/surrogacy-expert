    Surrogacy Laws in Mississippi: Guide for Intended Parents

    Mississippi presents complex legal terrain for gestational surrogacy. Surrogacy contracts have uncertain enforceability, and pre-birth orders are not reliably available.

    Legal Framework

    Mississippi does not have a gestational surrogacy statute, and the enforceability of surrogacy contracts under Mississippi law is uncertain. Courts have not developed a well-established surrogacy framework comparable to surrogacy-friendly states.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Mississippi

    Pre-birth orders are not reliably available in Mississippi. Parental rights typically must be established through post-birth proceedings that can be lengthy and uncertain.

    Working with a Surrogate in Mississippi

    Intended parents with Mississippi-based surrogates should strongly consider whether the surrogate is willing to deliver in a neighboring state with a more established legal framework. Consult with both a Mississippi-licensed attorney and a surrogacy-experienced attorney in a nearby friendlier state.
