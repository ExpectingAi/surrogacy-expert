    Surrogacy Laws in Mississippi: Guide for Intended Parents

    Mississippi is one of the most legally complex surrogacy states in the US. No statute governs surrogacy, courts have very limited experience, and the conservative political and legal climate creates significant uncertainty — particularly for LGBTQ+ families. Mississippi surrogates typically deliver in Georgia or Tennessee.

    Legal Environment

    Mississippi has no surrogacy statute, very limited case law specifically addressing gestational surrogacy, and courts with almost no surrogacy experience. The state's conservative political and legal climate creates substantial uncertainty for all intended parents and very high risk for LGBTQ+ families. Mississippi reproductive attorneys with surrogacy experience are extremely rare.

    What Mississippi Surrogates Should Do

    Delivering outside Mississippi is strongly recommended. Georgia is the most common alternative — Fulton County (Atlanta) courts have significant surrogacy experience, and Atlanta has good obstetric infrastructure. Tennessee (Nashville, Davidson County) is another option, though Tennessee has its own complexities. An experienced Mississippi reproductive attorney will coordinate cross-border delivery logistics.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Mississippi presents very high legal risk for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families. Out-of-state delivery in a surrogacy-friendly jurisdiction is essential for any LGBTQ+ family with a Mississippi surrogate.

    Key Steps

    If your surrogate is in Mississippi, retain attorneys licensed in Mississippi and your chosen delivery state immediately. Do not attempt to process parentage in Mississippi courts. An experienced reproductive attorney will guide you to the appropriate out-of-state delivery strategy.

    Mississippi surrogacy requires out-of-state delivery. Georgia (Atlanta) is the most commonly used alternative. Retain experienced counsel in both states from the very beginning.
