    Legal Framework

    Alaska does not have a comprehensive gestational surrogacy statute. Surrogacy arrangements are governed by general contract principles and case law. Gestational surrogacy contracts are not explicitly prohibited in Alaska, but their enforceability depends on the presiding judge and county.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Alaska

    Pre-birth orders are not routinely available in Alaska. Parental rights are typically established through post-birth proceedings. Working with an Alaska-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match is essential to understand the specific process and timeline.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Alaska are more variable than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples and single parents should obtain specific legal guidance from an Alaska-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Alaska as a delivery state.

    Alaska is a moderate-complexity surrogacy state. Intended parents can complete successful journeys here with the right legal guidance.
