    Surrogacy Laws in Kentucky: Guide for Intended Parents

    Kentucky is a moderate surrogacy state governed by case law. Pre-birth orders have been issued in Jefferson County (Louisville) courts. Northern Kentucky surrogates near Cincinnati can deliver in Ohio, which has experienced courts in Hamilton County.

    Legal Framework

    Kentucky has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Kentucky's general parentage statutes (Kentucky Revised Statutes Chapter 199). Jefferson County (Louisville) courts have more surrogacy experience than rural Kentucky courts. Kentucky's conservative political climate creates uncertainty for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Kentucky

    Pre-birth orders have been obtained in Jefferson County (Louisville) in surrogacy cases. Other Kentucky courts have limited experience. For Northern Kentucky surrogates, Hamilton County, Ohio (Cincinnati) courts have surrogacy experience and may be a cross-border delivery option worth discussing with your attorney.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Kentucky does not have explicit statutory protections for LGBTQ+ surrogacy families. The legal environment is more uncertain than in statute-based states. LGBTQ+ intended parents should consult carefully with a local reproductive attorney and evaluate cross-border delivery options where applicable.

    Key Steps

    Retain a Kentucky-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. Assess delivery county and cross-border options if applicable. Both parties must have independent legal representation.

    Louisville (Jefferson County) courts have the most surrogacy experience in Kentucky. Northern Kentucky surrogates should discuss Ohio delivery with their attorney as an option.
