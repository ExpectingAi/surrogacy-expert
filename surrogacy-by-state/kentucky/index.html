Surrogacy for Intended Parents in Kentucky

Kentucky has no surrogacy statute and limited case law. Some jurisdictions have been supportive; others less so. Not a recommended primary delivery state.

Legal Framework in Kentucky

Primary legal authority: Case law only — no statute

Kentucky's limited surrogacy case law and lack of experienced practitioners make it a challenging delivery state.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate.

Pre-Birth Orders in Kentucky

Pre-birth order availability: Limited — varies

Pre-birth orders have been issued in some Kentucky jurisdictions but are not reliably available statewide. Post-birth proceedings may be required depending on county.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Kentucky

LGBTQ+ friendly: Limited

Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Kentucky are more variable than in explicitly inclusive states. Consult a Kentucky-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Kentucky as a delivery state.

Choosing Kentucky as Your Delivery State

If your surrogate is in Kentucky, consult with a Kentucky-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. Ohio — while also moderate — has somewhat more established surrogacy case law and more experienced practitioners in the Cincinnati area.
