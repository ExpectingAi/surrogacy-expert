Legal Framework in Alabama

Primary law: No statute, limited case law

Alabama has no surrogacy statute and limited case law. Post-birth adoption or parentage action typically required. Intended parents considering Alabama as a delivery state should work with a reproductive attorney licensed in Alabama who has specific experience in gestational surrogacy arrangements.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate. Your surrogate does not need to live in the most favorable state — but she does need to deliver there.

Pre-Birth Orders in Alabama

Pre-birth order availability: No (Post-birth only).

A pre-birth order (PBO) is a court order issued during the pregnancy that names intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital to list them on the birth certificate at birth. Pre-birth orders in surrogacy-friendly states eliminate the need for post-birth adoption proceedings and provide the clearest legal path to parenthood.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Alabama

LGBTQ+ friendly: Limited. Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Alabama are less certain than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples or single parents should seek specific legal guidance from a Alabama-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Alabama as a delivery state.

Working with a Surrogate in Alabama

If your surrogate lives in Alabama, you'll need a reproductive attorney licensed in Alabama to draft your gestational carrier agreement and file for parental rights. Your fertility clinic may also have monitoring requirements at local clinics near your surrogate's home. Your agency will coordinate the logistics.
