    Legal Framework

    Alabama does not have a comprehensive gestational surrogacy statute. Surrogacy arrangements in Alabama are governed by case law and general contract principles. Gestational surrogacy contracts are not explicitly authorized by statute, but they are not prohibited either. Courts have generally enforced gestational surrogacy agreements when they are properly documented.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Alabama

    Pre-birth orders are not routinely available in Alabama. Intended parents typically establish parental rights through a post-birth adoption or parentage proceeding. The county in which the birth occurs and the presiding judge can significantly affect the process and timeline. Working with an Alabama-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your surrogate match is essential.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Alabama are more variable than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples and single parents should obtain specific legal guidance from an Alabama-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Alabama as a delivery state.

    Working with a Surrogate in Alabama

    If your surrogate is based in Alabama, consult with an Alabama-licensed reproductive attorney who has specific gestational surrogacy experience before finalizing your match. Understanding exactly what the legal process will look like in Alabama before you commit is essential.

    Alabama is a moderate-complexity surrogacy state. Intended parents can complete successful journeys here, but should retain experienced local legal counsel before proceeding.
