    Surrogacy Laws in Alabama: Guide for Intended Parents

    Alabama is a moderate-complexity surrogacy state governed by case law with no dedicated statute. Pre-birth orders have been issued in some courts, but the legal landscape is less predictable than in statute-based states, and LGBTQ+ families face additional uncertainty.

    Legal Framework

    Alabama has no surrogacy-specific statute. Gestational surrogacy is governed by case law and Alabama's general parentage statutes. Courts in Alabama have issued parentage orders for intended parents in gestational surrogacy cases, but there is no statewide uniformity. Jefferson County (Birmingham) and Madison County (Huntsville) courts have more experience with surrogacy than rural Alabama courts. Alabama's conservative legal and political climate creates more uncertainty for LGBTQ+ families than in statute-based states.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Alabama

    Pre-birth orders have been issued in Alabama in some courts, particularly in the Birmingham and Huntsville metro areas. PBOs are not routinely available statewide, and some courts may require post-birth proceedings instead. If your surrogate is in a rural Alabama county, your attorney will assess whether delivery in a more experienced jurisdiction is advisable.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Alabama's legal environment for LGBTQ+ families in surrogacy is more uncertain than in explicitly protective states. LGBTQ+ intended parents with Alabama surrogates should consult carefully with a local reproductive attorney and evaluate whether an out-of-state delivery in a more protective jurisdiction is feasible.

    Key Steps

    Retain an Alabama-licensed reproductive attorney before finalizing your match. Your attorney will assess the county, advise on delivery strategy, draft or review the GCA, and manage parentage proceedings. Both parties must have independent legal representation.

    Alabama surrogacy journeys are achievable but require an experienced local reproductive attorney and careful county selection. Evaluate neighboring Tennessee and Georgia for delivery if your surrogate is near a state line.
