Legal Framework in Rhode Island

Primary law: RIGL §15-8.1 (2020)

Rhode Island enacted modern surrogacy legislation in 2020 that explicitly covers all family types and allows pre-birth orders. Intended parents considering Rhode Island as a delivery state should work with a reproductive attorney licensed in Rhode Island who has specific experience in gestational surrogacy arrangements.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate. Your surrogate does not need to live in the most favorable state — but she does need to deliver there.

Pre-Birth Orders in Rhode Island

Pre-birth order availability: Yes (Pre-birth, all types).

A pre-birth order (PBO) is a court order issued during the pregnancy that names intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital to list them on the birth certificate at birth. Pre-birth orders in surrogacy-friendly states eliminate the need for post-birth adoption proceedings and provide the clearest legal path to parenthood.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Rhode Island

LGBTQ+ friendly: Yes. Rhode Island is explicitly inclusive of all family types in its surrogacy legal framework. Same-sex couples, single parents, and transgender intended parents can pursue gestational surrogacy with the same legal protections as all other intended parents.

Working with a Surrogate in Rhode Island

If your surrogate lives in Rhode Island, you'll need a reproductive attorney licensed in Rhode Island to draft your gestational carrier agreement and file for parental rights. Your fertility clinic may also have monitoring requirements at local clinics near your surrogate's home. Your agency will coordinate the logistics.
