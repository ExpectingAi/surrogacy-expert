Legal Framework in Pennsylvania

Primary law: Case law only, no statute

Pennsylvania has no surrogacy statute but courts generally uphold GCAs and issue pre-birth orders. Outcomes can vary by county/judge. Intended parents considering Pennsylvania as a delivery state should work with a reproductive attorney licensed in Pennsylvania who has specific experience in gestational surrogacy arrangements.

💡 The state where your surrogate delivers determines which laws govern your pre-birth order and birth certificate. Your surrogate does not need to live in the most favorable state — but she does need to deliver there.

Pre-Birth Orders in Pennsylvania

Pre-birth order availability: Yes (Pre-birth (varies by county)).

A pre-birth order (PBO) is a court order issued during the pregnancy that names intended parents as the legal parents and directs the hospital to list them on the birth certificate at birth. Pre-birth orders in surrogacy-friendly states eliminate the need for post-birth adoption proceedings and provide the clearest legal path to parenthood.

LGBTQ+ Intended Parents in Pennsylvania

LGBTQ+ friendly: Moderate. Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Pennsylvania are less certain than in explicitly inclusive states. Same-sex couples or single parents should seek specific legal guidance from a Pennsylvania-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Pennsylvania as a delivery state.

Working with a Surrogate in Pennsylvania

If your surrogate lives in Pennsylvania, you'll need a reproductive attorney licensed in Pennsylvania to draft your gestational carrier agreement and file for parental rights. Your fertility clinic may also have monitoring requirements at local clinics near your surrogate's home. Your agency will coordinate the logistics.
