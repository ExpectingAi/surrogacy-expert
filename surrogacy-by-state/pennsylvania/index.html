    Pennsylvania has a moderate surrogacy legal environment governed by case law. Pre-birth orders are available in many counties, and Pennsylvania has a substantial surrogate population.

    Pre-Birth Orders in Pennsylvania

    Pre-birth orders may be available in Pennsylvania depending on the county. Philadelphia and the surrounding suburbs have more surrogacy-experienced courts than rural counties. A Pennsylvania-licensed reproductive attorney can advise on county-specific practices before you finalize your delivery plan.

    LGBTQ+ Intended Parents

    Legal protections for LGBTQ+ intended parents in Pennsylvania are more variable than in explicitly inclusive states. LGBTQ+ parents should consult a Pennsylvania-licensed reproductive attorney before choosing Pennsylvania as a delivery state.

    Working with a Surrogate in Pennsylvania

    Pennsylvania has a large surrogate population, particularly in the Philadelphia metro and Pittsburgh areas. Retain a Pennsylvania-licensed reproductive attorney with specific gestational surrogacy experience.

    Pennsylvania is a moderate-complexity surrogacy state with a large surrogate population and active legal community, particularly in the Philadelphia metro area.
