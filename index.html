    The basics

    What is gestational surrogacy?

    In gestational surrogacy, a surrogate carries a pregnancy using an embryo created through IVF — she has no genetic connection to the child. Intended parents may use their own eggs and sperm, donor eggs, donor sperm, or both. The surrogate is not the biological mother.

    The journey

    How surrogacy works, start to finish

    Every journey is different — but the structure is predictable. Here's what to expect.

    Full timeline guide
    1

    Research & legal consult

    Learn the landscape and retain a reproductive attorney before signing with any agency.

    2

    Choose an agency & match

    Your agency recruits and vets surrogate candidates. Matching typically takes 1–4 months.

    3

    Legal contracts (GCA)

    Both parties retain independent attorneys and execute the Gestational Carrier Agreement.

    4

    IVF & embryo transfer

    The fertility clinic manages the FET protocol after screening and legal clearance. A positive beta confirms implantation.

    5

    Pregnancy & parentage order

    Your attorney files for the parentage order when available. Birth-certificate procedure depends on the delivery state.
