The Surrogate Matching Process for Intended Parents

Matching with the right surrogate is one of the most meaningful moments in your surrogacy journey. Here is how the process works and what to look for.

How Agencies Facilitate Matching

After your application is approved, your agency creates your intended parent profile and presents it to available surrogates whose stated preferences align with yours. Most agencies use a combination of criteria: geographic preferences, desired contact level during pregnancy, views on selective reduction, relationship style, and logistical factors like willingness to travel to a different state for delivery.

What a Surrogate Profile Includes

  • Personal background, family situation, and why she wants to be a surrogate
  • Complete obstetric history — prior pregnancies, deliveries, and outcomes
  • General health history and lifestyle information
  • Background check summary
  • Psychological evaluation summary
  • Home situation and support system
  • Preferences for contact during and after the pregnancy
  • Views on selective reduction and termination in specific circumstances
  • Personal photos and, in some cases, a video introduction

Reviewing a Profile: What to Focus On

Beyond the medical checklist, focus on alignment of values and expectations. The most common sources of conflict in surrogacy relationships are things that weren't discussed before matching: how often you expect communication, whether you'll attend appointments, what contact looks like after birth, and how decisions are made during the pregnancy.

💡 It's entirely appropriate to decline a match that doesn't feel right — even if the surrogate meets every medical and background criterion. Your instincts about fit and compatibility are valid data.

The First Meeting

Once both parties express interest, your agency facilitates a first meeting — usually a video call. Good topics:

  • What motivated her to become a surrogate?
  • What level of communication does she prefer — how often, by which method?
  • Is she open to intended parents attending key ultrasounds?
  • What are her expectations for the birth and the immediate postpartum period?
  • What does she envision for the relationship after the birth?
  • What are her views on selective reduction or termination in specific medical circumstances?

After the Match: What Happens Next

Once both parties agree to proceed, you are officially matched. Next steps: both parties retain separate reproductive attorneys and begin GCA negotiation; escrow account is established and funded; surrogate begins her comprehensive medical evaluation at your fertility clinic.
