The Surrogate Matching Process for Intended Parents

Matching with the right surrogate is one of the most meaningful moments in your surrogacy journey. Here is how the process works and what to expect.

How Agencies Match Intended Parents and Surrogates

Reputable agencies use a combination of criteria to facilitate matches: geographic preference, communication style preferences, level of desired contact during pregnancy, views on selective reduction and termination, relationship expectations (close/friendly vs. more formal), and logistical factors like whether the surrogate is willing to travel to a different state for delivery.

Reviewing Surrogate Profiles

Surrogate profiles typically include: personal background and family situation, prior pregnancy and delivery history, reasons for wanting to be a surrogate, lifestyle information (diet, smoking, alcohol), psychological evaluation results, background check summary, OB records from prior pregnancies, and personal photos. Agencies present profiles to intended parents whose preferences align with the surrogate's.

Take time with each profile. It is entirely appropriate to decline a match that doesn't feel right — and your agency should support that decision without pressure.

💡 One of the most important matching criteria is your mutual expectations about contact during the pregnancy and after birth. Discuss this openly with your surrogate before finalizing the match.

The First Meeting

Once both parties express interest, agencies typically facilitate a call or video meeting between intended parents and the prospective surrogate. This is your opportunity to ask questions, get a sense of each other's personalities, and assess fit. Topics to cover: how often you'd like updates, attendance at ultrasound appointments, presence at birth, communication method preferences, and what happens if medical complications arise.

When to Say Yes

There is no perfect surrogate, but there is a right match. You're looking for someone whose values, communication style, and expectations align with yours. Trust your instincts — if something feels off in the initial meeting, it's valid to decline. Your agency's job is to help you find a genuine fit, not to pressure you into moving forward quickly.
