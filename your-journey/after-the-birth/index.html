After the Birth: Life as New Parents After Surrogacy

Birth day arrives — and a new chapter begins. Here is what intended parents need to know about the days and weeks after their surrogate delivers.

The Hospital Stay

In the days after birth, your focus is entirely on your new baby. Your surrogate typically recovers in a separate room, unless you've all agreed to a different arrangement. The nursing staff at surrogacy-experienced hospitals understands this dynamic and will ensure you have appropriate access to your baby in the nursery or your designated room.

Finalizing Parental Rights

If a pre-birth order was issued, your names appear on the birth certificate immediately. If your state requires a post-birth process, your attorney will file the necessary paperwork within days of birth. The timeline varies by state — in some, it's completed before you leave the hospital; in others, it takes a few weeks.

Your Relationship with Your Surrogate After Birth

The journey doesn't end at birth — for most intended parents and surrogates, it transitions. Many maintain meaningful long-term relationships: exchanging updates on the child's milestones, celebrating birthdays, meeting in person. What the relationship looks like going forward should be discussed honestly before delivery, and treated with the same care you brought to every other stage of the journey.

💡 Many families send their surrogates annual birthday photos of the child. It's a small gesture that carries enormous meaning for the surrogate and honors the profound role she played in your family's story.

Adjusting to Parenthood

New parenthood after surrogacy comes with the same joys and challenges as any new parenthood — plus the emotional weight of an extraordinary journey to get there. Give yourself permission to feel every part of it. Connect with other intended parents through surrogacy community groups, and don't hesitate to work with a therapist experienced in fertility and family building.
