How Much Does Surrogacy Cost? Full Breakdown for Intended Parents

Gestational surrogacy in the United States typically costs between $80,000 and $180,000 in total. Here is a transparent, line-by-line breakdown of every major cost category — and what drives the variation.

The Full Cost Breakdown

Every surrogacy journey is different, but the major cost categories are consistent. Here is what to budget for:

💡 Surrogate compensation and health insurance are the two most variable line items. A surrogate with prior journeys in California will cost more than a first-time surrogate in a lower-cost state — but experience often translates to smoother journeys.

Surrogate Compensation: What Goes Into It

Base compensation is only part of what surrogates receive. The full compensation package typically includes:

  • Base fee ($30K–$60K paid in monthly installments during pregnancy)
  • Embryo transfer fees ($750–$1,500 per transfer)
  • Monthly allowance for incidentals ($200–$300/month)
  • Maternity clothing allowance (~$750)
  • Travel reimbursement for all medical appointments
  • Lost wages if surrogate must miss work for medical reasons
  • Housekeeping if bedrest is required
  • Life insurance policy (typically $250K–$500K term)
  • C-section fee if applicable (~$2,500–$4,000)
  • Multiples fee if twins/triplets ($5,000–$10,000 additional)

IVF & Medical Costs

Medical costs depend on how many embryo transfer cycles are needed and whether you're using donor eggs. A single frozen embryo transfer (FET) cycle typically costs $5,000–$8,000 beyond the base monitoring. If you're using donor eggs, add $25,000–$45,000 for the egg donation process. Your fertility clinic will provide a detailed treatment plan with cost estimates before you begin.

Do Insurance Costs Vary by State?

Yes, significantly. Some surrogates have employer-sponsored health insurance that includes surrogacy coverage (rare but valuable). Others have policies that explicitly exclude surrogacy. In many cases, intended parents must purchase a specific surrogacy-friendly health insurance policy for their carrier, which can cost $15,000–$30,000 annually. Your agency or a fertility insurance specialist can help navigate this.

Financing Your Journey

Surrogacy financing options include: personal savings or 401k loans, home equity loans or lines of credit, personal loans from specialty lenders (CapexMD, Prosper Healthcare Lending), fertility grants from organizations like the Baby Quest Foundation, and military-specific programs. Many intended parents combine several of these sources.
