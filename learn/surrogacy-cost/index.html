How Much Does Surrogacy Cost? Complete Guide for Intended Parents

Gestational surrogacy in the United States typically costs between $80,000 and $180,000 in total. Here is an honest, line-by-line breakdown of every major cost — and what drives the variation.

Total Cost Overview

There is no single number for surrogacy costs because every journey is different. Your total will depend on your state, your agency, your surrogate's compensation and health insurance situation, how many IVF cycles are needed, and your legal team's fees. That said, most intended parents should budget somewhere between $80,000 and $180,000.

Surrogate Compensation: The Full Picture

Surrogate base compensation is just the starting point. The full compensation package typically includes:

  • Base fee: $30,000–$60,000 paid in monthly installments during pregnancy.
  • Embryo transfer fee: $750–$1,500 per transfer attempt
  • Monthly allowance: $200–$300/month for incidental pregnancy expenses
  • Maternity clothing: Typically a one-time $750–$1,000 allowance
  • Travel reimbursement: Mileage or flights to all medical appointments
  • Lost wages: If medical appointments or bed rest require time off work
  • Childcare reimbursement: For the surrogate's children during appointments
  • Housekeeping: Typically $250–$300/week if bed rest is ordered
  • C-section fee: Additional $2,500–$4,000 if cesarean section is required
  • Multiples fee: Additional $5,000–$10,000 if twins or triplets are confirmed
  • Life insurance premium: $250,000–$500,000 term policy, paid by intended parents

IVF and Medical Costs

Medical costs depend heavily on how many embryo transfers are needed and whether you're using donor eggs:

  • Frozen embryo transfer (FET) cycle: $5,000–$8,000 per attempt, plus medications (~$3,000–$5,000)
  • Surrogate medical screening: $2,000–$4,000 for the comprehensive fertility clinic evaluation
  • Donor egg cycle (if needed): $25,000–$45,000 additional (egg donor compensation + agency fee + retrieval)

💡 If your embryo transfer fails and a second cycle is needed, budget for an additional $8,000–$15,000. Most intended parents have at least one or two transfers before achieving a confirmed pregnancy.

Health Insurance: The Wild Card

Surrogate health insurance is often the most unpredictable cost:

  • Best case: Surrogate has employer insurance covering gestational surrogacy. Intended parents pay only premiums.
  • Common case: Policy must be reviewed by a fertility insurance specialist. If it excludes surrogacy, a specialized policy must be purchased — typically $15,000–$30,000/year.
  • Worst case: A comprehensive surrogacy-specific policy required at maximum cost.

Always have a fertility insurance specialist review your surrogate's policy before committing to a match.

Financing Options

  • Personal savings or retirement account loans (401k)
  • Home equity loans or lines of credit (HELOC)
  • Specialty fertility/surrogacy lending (CapexMD, Prosper Healthcare Lending, Future Family)
  • Family loans
  • Fertility grants (Baby Quest Foundation, Pay It Forward Fertility Foundation, Men Having Babies)
  • Employer fertility benefits — increasingly common at major employers
