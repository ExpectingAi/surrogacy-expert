How to Find a Surrogate Mother as Intended Parents

Finding the right surrogate is one of the most important steps in your journey. Here is how most intended parents connect with their gestational carrier.

Working Through a Surrogacy Agency

The most common path to finding a surrogate is through a professional surrogacy agency. Agencies maintain pools of pre-screened, available surrogates and actively recruit new candidates. After you complete your agency application and create an intended parent profile, the agency presents you with surrogate profiles that match your preferences. This typically takes 1–4 months.

Known Surrogates

Some intended parents are approached by — or seek out — a friend, sister, or other known person who offers to carry for them. Known surrogate arrangements can be deeply meaningful but also require the same professional structure (independent attorneys, complete medical and psychological screening, formal GCA) as any other arrangement. The emotional complexity of a known surrogacy often makes professional support even more important.

Independent Matching

Online communities (surrogacy Facebook groups, Reddit communities, specialty matching platforms) allow intended parents and surrogates to find each other independently. This approach requires more due diligence — you are responsible for verifying the surrogate's history, arranging all screenings, and coordinating legal and medical professionals — but it can reduce costs and give you more control over the matching process.

⚠️ Never enter into a surrogacy arrangement without a formal gestational carrier agreement drafted by independent reproductive attorneys for both parties, regardless of how the match originated.

What Makes a Good Match?

Beyond the legal and medical checklist, the best matches share aligned values, realistic expectations, and compatible communication styles. The most common points of potential conflict: frequency of contact during pregnancy, presence at appointments and birth, views on selective reduction or termination, and what contact looks like after the birth. Discuss these openly before finalizing your match.
