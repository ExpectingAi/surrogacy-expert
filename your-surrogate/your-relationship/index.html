Your Relationship with Your Surrogate: Building a Healthy Partnership

Your relationship with your surrogate is unlike any other human connection. Here is how to build it with honesty, respect, and genuine care.

Alignment Before Matching

The most important investment in your surrogate relationship happens before you officially match. Use the matching process to have honest conversations about:

  • Communication: How often? By text, call, video? What's the expected response time?
  • Appointments: Which ones do you want to attend? In person or virtually?
  • Updates: How quickly after appointments do you want to hear news?
  • Complications: How and when would she notify you if something went wrong?
  • Birth: Do you want to be in the delivery room? Who else will she have there?
  • Post-birth: What does she envision the relationship looking like after delivery?

Misalignment on these expectations is the most common source of tension in surrogacy relationships. Address them explicitly — not just once, but revisit them as the pregnancy progresses.

Communication During Pregnancy

The communication pattern that works best varies between relationships. Some intended parents and surrogates text daily and become close friends; others check in weekly and maintain a warm, professional dynamic. Both are completely valid. What matters is that both parties feel comfortable with the level of contact.

  • Lead with gratitude and recognition — not just with medical questions
  • Ask about her life, her family, and how she's feeling physically and emotionally
  • Be responsive when she reaches out
  • Send updates about your life too — let her feel connected to the family she's growing

💡 The most meaningful gestures are often the simplest: a handwritten thank-you note, a care package in the third trimester, or a thoughtful message after a difficult appointment.

Navigating Disagreements

Even the best relationships encounter friction. Common tension points include: frequency of communication, diet or lifestyle choices during pregnancy, and differences in how pregnancy news is shared publicly. Address these directly and respectfully. Your agency case manager is a valuable mediator — involve them if communication breaks down.

After the Birth

The post-birth relationship is one of the most personal aspects of any surrogacy journey. Some pairs become lifelong friends. Others exchange annual updates. All outcomes are valid — what matters is that both parties feel honored and respected in whatever form the relationship takes.
