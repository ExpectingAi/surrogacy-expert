Your Relationship with Your Surrogate: What to Expect

Your relationship with your surrogate is one of the most unique human connections you will ever experience. Here is how to nurture it with care and clarity.

Setting Expectations Before You Match

The foundation of a healthy surrogate relationship is honest conversation before you ever match. The key topics to align on: how frequently you'll communicate and by what means, whether you'll attend appointments (in person or virtually), how you want to be told about pregnancy milestones and any complications, what your surrogate's preferences are around visitors and birth support, and what contact looks like after the birth.

Communication During Pregnancy

Most intended parent-surrogate pairs settle into a comfortable rhythm fairly quickly — regular texts, weekly check-ins, sharing ultrasound images and pregnancy updates. Some pairs become genuinely close; others maintain a warm but more formal relationship. Both are valid. What matters is that both parties feel comfortable with the level of contact and honored in the relationship.

💡 Small acts of thoughtfulness go a long way: a care package during the first trimester when nausea is rough, a gift card for a pregnancy massage in the third trimester, a sincere thank-you note after a difficult appointment. These gestures matter more than most intended parents realize.

Navigating Differences and Conflict

Even in the best surrogacy relationships, differences of opinion can arise — about diet during pregnancy, travel, work decisions, or communication preferences. Address differences early, calmly, and directly. Your agency case manager is a valuable resource for mediation. Never let a small tension fester into a larger conflict.

After the Birth

Many intended parents and surrogates maintain meaningful relationships long after the birth — exchanging holiday photos, celebrating milestones, and in some cases becoming genuine friends. The nature of the post-birth relationship should be discussed honestly before delivery and approached with the same care and respect you brought to every other part of the journey.
