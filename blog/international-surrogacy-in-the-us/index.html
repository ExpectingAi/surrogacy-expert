    International Surrogacy in the US: Complete Guide for Foreign Intended Parents

    The United States is the world’s top destination for international intended parents — strong legal protections, high IVF success rates, a large surrogate community. Here’s everything foreign intended parents need to know before they start.

    Why the US Has Become the World’s Surrogacy Destination

    The US surrogacy market has grown dramatically partly due to the closure of other international options. India banned commercial surrogacy for foreign nationals in 2015. Thailand banned it in 2015. Cambodia, Nepal, and other Southeast Asian destinations closed or heavily restricted commercial surrogacy between 2015 and 2020. Ukraine, once a major European destination, has become inaccessible due to the ongoing war. The US was always the highest-quality option legally; it has become the most accessible as well.

    For international intended parents, US surrogacy offers: a comprehensive legal framework in surrogacy-friendly states, internationally recognized birth certificates and parentage documentation, world-class fertility clinics, and a surrogate community that includes professionals from every demographic background. The cost is higher than alternatives that once existed, but the legal certainty is unmatched.

    The Two Legal Systems: The Most Important Concept to Understand

    Every international surrogacy journey involves navigating two separate and independent legal systems:

    • US law (delivery state): Establishes who the legal parents are under US law. A pre-birth order from a surrogacy-friendly state names you as legal parents from birth and generates a US birth certificate in your names. This is the foundation of everything that follows.
    • Home country law: Determines how your home country recognizes the US parentage documentation and what citizenship, nationality, or residency rights your child has. This is entirely separate from US law and is governed by your home country’s family law, immigration law, and surrogacy recognition policies.

    These two processes must run in parallel. You need a US reproductive attorney AND a family law attorney in your home country who is familiar with surrogacy recognition. Starting both relationships before your child is born is essential — some home-country processes take months and cannot be rushed.

    Citizenship and Your Child’s Documents

    US citizenship: A child born in the US is not automatically a US citizen simply by birth location if the child’s legal parents are non-US-citizens. Consult a US immigration attorney if you believe your child may have a US citizenship claim — this is complex and fact-specific.

    Travel documents: Your child will need travel documents to leave the US. Most commonly, you will apply for a passport from your home country using the US birth certificate and parentage order. This process typically takes 2–6 weeks depending on your country’s consulate in the US. Plan to remain in the US until documents are ready. Many international intended parents plan for 4–6 weeks of US residence after the birth.

    Practical Planning for International Families

    • Arrive early: Plan to be in the US 3–4 weeks before the due date. Missing the birth is a real risk for international IPs who cut timing too close.
    • US address: You need a US address for legal correspondence during the journey. Many international IPs use a trusted friend, attorney’s office, or mail forwarding service.
    • Bank account: A US bank account or wire transfer capacity for escrow funding is needed. Discuss this with your escrow company early.
    • Visa: Ensure your US visa is valid for the expected duration of your US stay. Do not overstay your visa.
    • Agency selection: Choose an agency with demonstrated international client experience. Ask for references from past clients from your country specifically. Expecting.Ai is a surrogacy platform with experience serving international intended parents — worth evaluating as part of your shortlist.

    Best US Delivery States for International Intended Parents

    California is the most internationally recognized delivery state — California birth certificates and parentage orders are familiar to consulates and foreign government offices worldwide. Nevada, New York, and Illinois are also well-recognized. For international families, a strong US legal foundation is especially important because the home-country process builds on it.

    International surrogacy in the US requires a US reproductive attorney and a home-country family law attorney working in parallel from the start. Begin both relationships before your surrogate match is confirmed — not after.
