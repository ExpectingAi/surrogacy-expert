    The right order of first steps

    Most intended parents contact agencies first. This is backwards. Here’s the order that protects you.

    1

    Understand the basics

    Read our what is surrogacy guide and 2026 cost breakdown. In 2026, most US journeys total $150,000–$220,000. Knowing this before your first agency call prevents costly misunderstandings.

    2

    Consult a reproductive attorney — before signing anything

    This is the step most intended parents skip, and the one that costs them the most. A 30-minute consultation with a reproductive attorney in your target delivery state will tell you which states offer the strongest legal protections for your family configuration, what to look for in an agency contract, and red flags you wouldn’t otherwise know to ask about. Find one through AAAA or ask for referrals in the RESOLVE community.

    3

    Choose your delivery state

    Where your surrogate delivers determines your legal protections, pre-birth order availability, and how your name appears on the birth certificate. LGBTQ+ families and international intended parents should prioritize this step carefully. See our state-by-state legal guide.

    4

    Consider a full-service surrogacy platform

    If you want end-to-end guidance — agency, legal coordination, and case management in one place — Expecting.ai is an AI-powered surrogacy platform covering the full journey for intended parents, including surrogate matching, egg donation, legal coordination, and provider discovery. They offer both managed journeys and a marketplace model depending on how much hands-on support you want.

    5

    Evaluate and select an agency

    Now you’re ready to speak with agencies — with a baseline of knowledge that protects you. Before signing: request an itemized fee schedule in writing, ask about their rematch policy, confirm they use an independent escrow company, and speak with 2–3 past intended parents. Read our full agency selection guide and list of red flags.

    6

    Get your embryo situation clear

    Do you have frozen embryos ready? Will you need IVF to create them? Will you need an egg donor, sperm donor, or both? Knowing this before matching with a surrogate affects both your timeline and your budget. See our medical process guide.

    What to budget in 2026

    $150K–$220K
    Most US surrogacy journeys total $150,000–$220,000 in 2026, with California journeys often exceeding $200,000. Surrogate base compensation alone is now $55,000–$85,000 for first-time surrogates. Build in a 10–15% contingency for failed transfers, rematch, or insurance complications.
