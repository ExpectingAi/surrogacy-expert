Single Parent Surrogacy: A Guide for Single Intended Parents

Single parenthood through surrogacy is more common than most people realize — and the process is largely the same as for couples. Here is what single intended parents need to know.

Single Men

Single men pursuing surrogacy need both an egg donor and a surrogate. The processes run largely in parallel — you can begin searching for an egg donor through your fertility clinic's donor database while your agency works on finding a surrogate match. Sperm is used to fertilize donor eggs in the lab, creating embryos that are then transferred to your surrogate. Legal parentage is established through a pre-birth order in surrogacy-friendly states, with the intended father listed as the sole parent on the birth certificate.

Single Women

Single women who pursue surrogacy typically do so because they cannot safely carry a pregnancy themselves — due to medical conditions, uterine absence, or health risk. They may use their own eggs (fertilized with donor sperm) or donor eggs. The surrogacy process is the same as for couples. Legal parentage is established through a pre-birth or post-birth legal process depending on the state.

💡 In most surrogacy-friendly states, being single is not a legal barrier to being listed as a child's sole parent through surrogacy. Your reproductive attorney will confirm what the process looks like in your chosen state.

Practical Considerations for Single Parents

As a single intended parent, planning ahead for the postpartum period is especially important — since you will not have a co-parent to share early newborn care. Build your support system before birth: family, friends, a night nurse or postpartum doula, and a pediatrician you trust. Single parents consistently report that the preparation paid off.

Finding the Right Agency

Choose an agency with demonstrated experience working with single intended parents. Most reputable agencies are fully inclusive and have worked with single IPs many times. Ask directly about their experience and whether single IPs represent a meaningful portion of their completed journeys.
