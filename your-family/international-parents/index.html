    International Intended Parents: Surrogacy in the US

    The United States is the world’s top surrogacy destination for international intended parents — combining a strong legal framework, high IVF success rates, and accessible logistics.

    Why International Parents Choose the US

    The United States offers international intended parents several advantages that are difficult to find elsewhere: explicit statutory frameworks in multiple states, pre-birth orders available for all family types, high-quality fertility clinics with strong IVF success rates, no residency requirement, English-language legal process (interpretable with assistance), and a robust community of professionals with international experience.

    Two Layers of Legal Process

    International intended parents must navigate two legal systems:

    • US law (delivery state): Establishes parental rights in the delivery state through a pre-birth or post-birth order. A US reproductive attorney handles this.
    • Home country law: Recognizes those US parental rights and confers citizenship or residency on the child. Requirements vary enormously by country. A family law or immigration attorney in the home country handles this.

    Both legal processes should be researched before beginning your US surrogacy journey. Some countries have significant obstacles to recognizing US surrogacy judgments or granting citizenship to surrogate-born children. Countries where international parents regularly complete US surrogacy successfully include: Israel, Australia, UK, Canada, Germany, France (via citizenship), China, Japan, and most of Latin America.

    The Child’s US Citizenship

    Children born in the United States to foreign intended parents are eligible for US citizenship by birth under the 14th Amendment if at least one intended parent is the genetic parent of the child and is a US citizen. For non-US-citizen intended parents, the child may be eligible for a US passport but citizenship rules are complex — consult with a US immigration attorney before the birth.

    Best Delivery States for International Parents

    California, Nevada, and Washington are the most popular delivery states for international intended parents. All three have explicit statutory frameworks, routinely issue pre-birth orders for all family types, have well-developed international surrogacy infrastructure, and are experienced with the logistics of international intended parents attending key appointments remotely.

    Working Remotely with a US Agency

    International intended parents typically handle most of the surrogacy journey remotely — matching via video call, signing legal documents electronically, monitoring via shared medical updates, and traveling to the US for embryo retrieval (if using own eggs or sperm) and for the birth. Most US agencies and fertility clinics are experienced with remote international intended parents and have established protocols to facilitate the process.

    The key to a smooth international journey: research your home country’s legal landscape before you start. Some countries have resolved this clearly; others require careful legal planning.
