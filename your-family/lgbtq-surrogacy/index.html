    LGBTQ+ Surrogacy: Building Your Family as an LGBTQ+ Intended Parent

    Complete guide to surrogacy for LGBTQ+ intended parents — same-sex male couples, same-sex female couples, transgender parents, legal protections by state, and what to expect.

    Gestational Surrogacy and LGBTQ+ Families

    Gestational surrogacy is the most common path to biological parenthood for LGBTQ+ individuals and couples. Because the surrogate has no genetic connection to the baby, gestational surrogacy is legally cleaner and emotionally clearer than any other arrangement involving third-party reproduction.

    Same-Sex Male Couples

    Same-sex male couples pursuing surrogacy need two things beyond what heterosexual couples need: an egg donor and a gestational surrogate. These two tracks can run simultaneously, and many fertility clinics and agencies have well-established protocols for coordinating them. One or both partners can contribute sperm, and it’s possible to create embryos from both partners and then decide which to transfer, or to create embryos from each partner separately and decide later. See our dedicated guide for same-sex male couples.

    Same-Sex Female Couples

    Same-sex female couples typically pursue surrogacy when: both partners are unable to carry a pregnancy, one partner cannot carry but the other can (in which case the one who cannot carry is typically the egg donor and the one who can carry acts as the gestational carrier), or for other medical reasons. Same-sex female couples who use one partner’s eggs and the other partner’s uterus are using a process called reciprocal IVF — not surrogacy.

    Transgender and Non-Binary Intended Parents

    Transgender women and non-binary individuals who have undergone gender-affirming surgery affecting reproductive organs may pursue surrogacy with donor eggs and their own sperm (if preserved pre-transition). Transgender men who have not undergone bottom surgery may be able to carry a pregnancy themselves or serve as egg donors. The medical and legal process varies significantly based on the specific situation — consulting both a reproductive endocrinologist and a reproductive attorney before starting is essential.

    Best States for LGBTQ+ Surrogacy

    The most protective states for LGBTQ+ surrogacy are: California, Nevada, Washington, Colorado, Illinois, New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, and Maine. All of these states issue pre-birth orders for all family types without distinction based on sexual orientation, gender identity, or marital status.

    States to Use Caution

    States where LGBTQ+ intended parents face more complex legal situations include Texas, Florida, and any state with restrictive statutory language around “married” or “heterosexual” intended parents. Working with an experienced reproductive attorney who specifically understands LGBTQ+ surrogacy in your target delivery state is essential.

    LGBTQ+ families build their families through surrogacy at the same rates as other intended parents. Choose your delivery state thoughtfully, and the legal process can be just as smooth.
