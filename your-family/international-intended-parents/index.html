International Intended Parents: Surrogacy in the US from Abroad

The United States is one of the world's most popular destinations for international intended parents pursuing gestational surrogacy, thanks to its established legal framework, experienced professionals, and high success rates.

Why International Parents Choose the United States

Many countries have restricted or banned commercial surrogacy for foreign nationals — including India, Thailand, Cambodia, Ukraine (due to the ongoing conflict), and Mexico. The US remains one of the most accessible, legally secure options for international intended parents, offering: a mature legal framework in surrogacy-friendly states, experienced agencies and fertility clinics accustomed to working with international clients, enforceability of pre-birth orders, and exceptionally high IVF success rates at top clinics.

Key Legal Considerations

International IPs face two layers of legal complexity: US law (establishing parental rights in the state where your surrogate delivers) and home-country law (how your country recognizes US-issued birth certificates and parentage orders, and what documentation your child needs to enter your country). You need experienced reproductive attorneys in both the US and your home country.

⚠️ Not all countries recognize parental rights established through surrogacy abroad equally. Israel, France, Germany, and Italy have specific legal processes for international surrogacy. Get home-country legal advice before you begin.

Citizenship for Your Baby

If at least one intended parent is a US citizen, the child may be eligible for US citizenship at birth regardless of where they are born. If neither parent is a US citizen, the child is born in the US but is not automatically a US citizen — they will be issued a birth certificate and you'll need to handle immigration documentation to bring them to your home country. Work with a US immigration attorney experienced in surrogacy alongside your reproductive attorney.

Choosing the Right State

California is the most popular state for international intended parents: it issues pre-birth orders for all family types regardless of genetic connection, has a well-developed infrastructure of agencies and clinics experienced with international clients, and has legal stability that has been consistent for decades. Nevada, Washington, and Connecticut are also strong options.

Working with a US Agency as an International IP

Most reputable US agencies are experienced with international intended parents and can guide you through the logistics of: remote meetings and communication, travel to the US for medical procedures and birth, remote signing of legal documents, banking and wire transfer protocols for escrow funding, and coordination with your home-country legal team.
