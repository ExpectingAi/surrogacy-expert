Surrogacy for Gay & Same-Sex Male Couples

For same-sex male couples, gestational surrogacy combined with egg donation is the primary path to having a biological child. Here is the full picture.

The Process: Two Parts Running in Parallel

Same-sex male couples pursuing surrogacy need two key components beyond the standard surrogacy process: an egg donor and a gestational surrogate. These are two separate people, two separate legal agreements, and two separate processes — but they're often coordinated in parallel to save time. Your fertility clinic and agency will help you manage both tracks simultaneously.

Choosing an Egg Donor

Egg donors are found through fertility clinic databases, independent egg donor agencies, or known donors (a family member or friend). You review profiles that typically include physical characteristics, education, health history, personality, and often childhood photos. Most clinics offer fresh or frozen donor egg options; frozen is typically faster and less expensive.

Which Partner's Sperm?

Same-sex male couples have several options: use one partner's sperm for all embryos, split the eggs and fertilize with each partner's sperm separately (creating two sets of embryos), or use both. Some couples choose to split eggs — transferring one embryo from each partner in separate cycles — so that each partner has a biological connection to a child. This is a deeply personal decision and should be discussed with your fertility clinic and with each other.

💡 Some clinics can identify which embryos carry which partner's genetics through preimplantation genetic testing (PGT). Others work with "blind" embryo assignment — not disclosing whose embryo is transferred. Discuss this preference with your REI early.

Legal Parental Rights for Both Partners

In most surrogacy-friendly states, both partners can be named as legal parents on the birth certificate through a pre-birth order, regardless of genetic connection. In some states, the non-genetic parent may need to complete a second-parent adoption. Work with a reproductive attorney experienced in same-sex surrogacy in your target state to ensure both partners are fully legally protected.

Best States for Gay Intended Parents

California, Nevada, Washington, Connecticut, Maine, Rhode Island, New Jersey, New York, and Illinois are among the most protective states for same-sex parents pursuing surrogacy. They issue pre-birth orders that name both partners regardless of genetics, and their laws are explicitly inclusive of all family structures.
