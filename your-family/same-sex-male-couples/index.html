    Surrogacy for Gay & Same-Sex Male Couples: Complete Guide

    Complete guide for same-sex male couples pursuing surrogacy — egg donation, sperm decisions, legal parental rights for both partners, and choosing the right delivery state.

    The Two Things Same-Sex Male Couples Need

    Two male partners need both a gestational surrogate and an egg donor to have a child through surrogacy. These two arrangements run in parallel and are coordinated by your fertility clinic and agency. The surrogate and egg donor are almost always different people.

    Sperm Decisions: One Partner or Both?

    One of the most personal decisions same-sex male couples face is whose sperm to use. Options include: one partner contributes sperm for this pregnancy (and potentially the other for a future sibling), both partners each fertilize half the retrieved eggs (creating embryos from each), or sperm from both partners is mixed and used together (leaving genetic parentage uncertain, which most couples prefer to avoid). There is no right answer — what matters is choosing the approach that feels right for your family.

    Egg Donation

    Egg donors can be found through the fertility clinic’s donor database, an independent egg donor agency, or known donors (friends or family). Frozen donor eggs are also increasingly available and can simplify coordination. When choosing an egg donor, consider health history, genetic screening, physical characteristics, and the level of contact (if any) you prefer.

    Legal Parenthood for Both Partners

    In surrogacy-friendly states, both partners can be named on the birth certificate via a pre-birth order, regardless of which partner is the genetic father. This is an important reason to choose your delivery state carefully: in states with weaker surrogacy frameworks, the non-genetic partner may need to complete a second-parent adoption after the birth. California, Nevada, Illinois, New York, New Jersey, Washington, and Colorado all reliably establish both partners’ parental rights via PBO.

    Best Delivery States for Gay Male Couples

    The strongest states for same-sex male couples are those with explicit, comprehensive surrogacy statutes that cover all family types: California, Nevada, Washington, Illinois, New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, Colorado, Oregon, and Maryland. All routinely issue PBOs naming both partners as parents from birth.

    The non-genetic partner’s parental rights are just as important as the genetic partner’s — protect them with the right delivery state and an experienced reproductive attorney.
